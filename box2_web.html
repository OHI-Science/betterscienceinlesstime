Better Science in Less Time

  • Use online resources for self-paced learning
    The books and courses above provide in-depth philosophies and are good for initial learning as well as for reference later on.

  • Join and/or create communities
    Watch a webinar with a friend or group during lunch or a happy hour, or discuss an academic article on these topics during lab meeting or journal club. Additionally, join or keep tabs on communities online, like #rstats on Twitter.

  • Ask for help
    Expect that someone has already asked your question — try pasting your error messages directly into Google.

  • Attend in-person workshops and conferences
    Software Carpentry and [Data Carpentry]http://www.datacarpentry.org/ run bootcamps that teach skills for research computing, and you can also attend conferences like useR both for skill-building and to learn how others are using these tools.

  • Watch presentations from past conferences
    More and more, slide decks and videos of presentations are appearing online. For example, you can see presentations from the the 2016 useR conference and the 2017 RStudio conference.

  • Read Blogs
    There are many individuals who blog about open data science concepts, R packages, workflows, etc. Try Googling a package you’re using, or going to the website of someone you are following on Twitter.
